Any kind of photography is prohibited during the hackathon. If someone is found doing so, they will be disqualified. This is done to protect the privacy and intellectual property of all participants.
01
Can I work on an existing project or does it have to be new?

All projects must be developed during the hackathon. Any plagiarism or reuse of previous work will result in disqualification, though you are allowed to use existing tools, frameworks, or concepts as a foundation.

02
What should I bring to a hackathon?

Youll need your laptop, chargers, and any other personal equipment you may need. Enthusiasm and creativity are a must!

03
Can I participate as a solo participant?

Yes, you can participate individually or in a team. Teams can consist of 1 to 4 members.

04
Is it my first time participating in a hackathon.Can i still register?

Absolutely! HackHaven is open to both first-timers and experienced hackers. Just register and dive in!

05
Is there a parking facility available for participants?

No, parking facility is not available for participants. Participants are advised to make alternative arrangements.

06
Can I form a team on-site?

Yes, you can form a team with other participants on-site. However, we encourage you to form teams in advance to ensure a smooth experience.

07
Are there any restrictions on the programming languages or technologies I can use?

No, there are no restrictions. You're free to use any programming language. However, personal network will be blocked using the firewall and internet access will be provided by the organizers to your systems.

08
How are hackathon projects judged?

Projects are typically judged on factors such as innovation, technical execution, usability, and presentation.

09
What are the benefits of participating in a hackathon?

Hackathons offer opportunities for learning, networking, and building your portfolio. They are also a great way to gain experience in rapid prototyping and agile development.
